RULES AND REGULATIONS

  • The Contest will be held in two rounds, the first being an online round to be conducted on 8th of March, 2020 and the second being an offline on-site round to be held at the university campus on 12th of March, 2020.

  • Please do not discuss strategy or algorithms used in your code during the live contest. This will cause disqualification from the contest, apart from seeming totally uncool.

  • Participants will be ranked according to the number of problems solved. Each problem will have weightage of one point, awarded only if the problem is fully solved (passing all test cases). No partial points will be awarded. Go all in, or go home.

  • Teams must consist of two people. Two many developers spoil the code.

  • Teams having same number of problems solved will be ranked according to the total time consumed, which is the sum of the time consumed for each problem solved. There is no time consumed for a problem that is not solved.

  • Plagiarism of any kind is totally prohibited, and any code found to be plagiarized will result in the disqualification of the team.
